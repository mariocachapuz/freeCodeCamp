Bio

George Orwell, pseudonym for Eric Arthur Blair, was an English novelist and critic born in 1903.
A man of strong opinions who wrote mostly about the political movements of his time, including imperialism and communism.

View bio on Wikipedia »

Books

Orwell didn't write solely novels, having add to his vast but relatively short life a number of essays, nonfiction and poems.
Two of the most well known Orwell's books are "Animal Farm" (1945) and "Nineteen Eighty-Four" (1949).

George Orwell Timeline

  • 1903 - Born in Motihari, India on June 25
  • 1904 - Moves with his family to England
  • 1921 - Is admitted in the Imperial Police
  • 1922 - Takes imperial police officer post in Burma
  • 1927 - Resigns and moves to London
  • 1928 - Moves to Paris to work as a journalist
  • 1929 - Returns to England and takes work as a private child tutor
  • 1931 - Becomes teacher at a school for boys in Hayes
  • 1933 - Publishes "Down and Out in Paris and London"
  • 1934 - Publishes "Burmese Days"
  • 1936 - Travels to Spain to take part in the Civil War
  • 1937 - Publishes "The Road to Wigan Pier"
  • 1938 - Publishes "Homage to Catalonia"
  • 1941 - Takes work with BBC's Eastern Service
  • 1944 - Adopts his only child, Richard
  • 1945 - Publishes "Animal Farm"
  • 1949 - Publishes "Nineteen Eighty-Four
  • 1950 - Dies of tuberculosis on January 21
